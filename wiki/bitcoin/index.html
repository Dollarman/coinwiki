    Bitcoin - BTC

    About:

    • Official website: https://bitcoincore.org/
    • Released on: 9 January 2009
    • Created by: Satoshi Nakamoto.
      тоб Note: Satoshi Nakamoto is a fake name that authored the original Bitcoin whitepaper and held a presence in online forums until December 13, 2010.
    • Country of origin: Unknown
    • Main purpose: Peer-to-Peer censorship-resistant currency.
    • Managing Organization: Bitcoin Core [link]
    • Type of Organization: Open Source project where core developers are sponsored by big companies. [link]
    • How much do developers own?: Satoshi may own up to 1 million BTC (5% of total supply), according to studies. Current developers are privately funded through corporate sponsorhip. [link]
    • Is it a derivative token of another network?: No.
    • Programmable with Smart Contracts: No. [link]
      тоб Note: Bitcoin allows scripting transactions with certain logical conditions, but they are very limited in scope. Read our post on Smart Contracts.
    • Privacy features: None. All transactions and wallets are public and searchable on block explorers.
    • Whitepaper: Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System

    Tech Specs:

    • Types of transactions: Only public transactions
    • Can it be mined?: Mining
    • Primary mining tool: ASICs. Mining Bitcoin using CPUs or GPUs is not profitable. Learn more about ASICs [link]
    • Mining Algorithm: SHA-256
    • Consensus protocol: Proof of Work

    Wallet Details:

    • Privacy: None. All transactions and wallets are public and searchable on block explorers.
    • Are public addresses reusable?: No. [link]
      тоб Note: Read our post on Reusable Addresses
    • Does it support multi-signature wallets?: true
    • How many public addresses can I make with 1 seed: 1
    • How many seeds are possible?: 2^128 [link]
    • Public Address format: 26-35 alphanumeric characters starting in 1, 3 or bc1 depending on the type of address. [link]
      тоб Note: P2PKH and P2SH addresses are case-sensitive and start with a 1 or a 3. Bech32 is a newer address type that is not case-sensitive and starts with bc1. Example: bc1qect6kk3naa94a09d8gwfydwzjc0c9wccxjv8vx
    • Seed format: 128-bit Seed Phrase composed of 12 words.

    Distribution of coin supply:

    • Mining: 100%
    • Staking (Proof of Stake): 0 %
    • Pre-release sale (ICO): 0 %
    • Free Distribution Event (Airdrop): 0 %
    • End date for full distribution: Mining will finish sometime during the year 2140
    • Current Supply: 17366475.0 BTC
      last updated: 2018-11-07
    • Total Supply: 21,000,000 BTC
